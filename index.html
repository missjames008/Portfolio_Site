Who Am I?

I am a multi-talented web developer with a diverse background and interests. I have traveled and lived all over the world, and speak English, French, Spanish, and some Arabic. I love doing anything artistic, especially if it relates to social justice and gender equality.

Why Web Development?

After earning my Bachelor's degree in sociology and my Master's in human rights with a focus on preventing violence against women, I noticed a gap in the market: a lack of tech knowledge and utilization in the nonprofit/NGO space. Realizing this, I undertook a four-month coding boot camp that would give me the tools I needed to fill this gap. With a preexisting passion for social justice and now possessing knowledge of multiple programming languages and databases, I am ready to help you and your organization take the next step into the digital space!

Group Project: VolunteerUP

This project was a collaboration between myself and five other individuals to produce a functioning web application that would connect people with local short-term volunteer opportunities. Utilizing Ruby on Rails, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, I contributed code for integrating login with Facebook and Twitter, breadcrumb navigation, styling and design, and bug fixes. Each piece of the project was also supported by tests written in RSpec and Capybara to ensure flexibility of code.

Art Portfolio

×

Blue Elephant

Inspired by traditional Indian mehendi (henna) designs, this work uses acrylics texturized with gel medium to create a layered silhouette that blends modern abstraction with traditional styles.

×

Weeping Woman

The background of this work is composed of newspaper headlines about acts of violence, in multiple languages, transferred by hand using an acid blending marker. The foreground silhouette, painted with acrylics, exemplifies the heartbreak caused by this worldwide violence.

×

Desert Rose

Inspired by the cover art for Paolo Coelho’s ‘The Zahir,’ this piece features newspaper cuttings as a background, all of which center around the topic of human rights violations. The foreground, an impressionistic rendering of a woman gazing at the horizon, evokes both freedom and sadness as the solitary figure illustrates both independence and loneliness.

×

There Must Be a Better Way

The outline of a man and a woman embracing is superimposed over newspaper and magazine clippings portraying the many negative aspects present in romantic relationships. The most prominent of these – cuttings spliced together to form the phrase ‘There must be a better way’ – indicates the hope that these unhealthy patterns do not have to persist if we are willing to rethink the way we relate to one another.
